# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

ios

## Users

A single person (you) tracking your own personal budget, with sports betting treated as a first-class budget category rather than bolted on. Used in the moment — checking the betting allowance before placing a bet, logging a bet in under 15 seconds right after placing it, glancing at net win/loss or which card to use for a purchase. No multi-user support and no auth; this is a personal daily-carry tool, not a product for other people yet.

## Product Purpose

Budgerr App is the phone-in-your-hand client for the Budgerr backend: a personal budgeting system that puts betting spend alongside groceries, dining, and every other category instead of hiding it in a separate app. It exists so betting money is visible, bounded, and accounted for the same way any other spending is — success looks like never being surprised by how much has gone into bets this month, and being able to log a bet faster than opening the sportsbook app itself.

## Positioning

The one place where your betting allowance and your actual budget live together — so a bet is just another budget line, not a separate, unaccounted-for habit.

## Brand Personality

Warm and reassuring. The tone should help you feel in control of betting spend rather than anxious or judged about it — no shame framing, no gamified excitement either. Status states (on track / near limit / over) should read like a calm check-in, not an alarm or a scoreboard.

## Anti-references

Not the generic fintech look — no navy-and-teal palette, no stacked gradient-metric cards, no Mint/Copilot-style dashboard sameness. Also avoid sportsbook/casino visual language (neon, celebratory odds badges, DraftKings/FanDuel energy) — this app's job is calm accounting, not encouraging more betting.

## Design Principles

- **Money is money.** A bet, a grocery run, and a dinner tab get the same visual treatment and the same seriousness — no separate "fun" styling for the betting category.
- **Calm over alarming.** Budget status (on track / warning / over limit) should read as reassurance-first, using tone and clarity rather than urgency or shame to communicate state.
- **Fast in the moment.** The core interaction — checking the allowance, logging a bet — happens standing in line or seconds after placing a bet. Every added tap or bit of visual noise works against that.
- **One person, no performance.** There's no audience to impress and no growth loop to design for. Optimize for daily-use clarity over acquisition-style polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline: solid text/background contrast and reduced-motion support. The existing success/warning/danger status colors should be distinguishable without relying on hue alone (pair with icon/label/shape, not color only), since they're the core signal for budget state.
